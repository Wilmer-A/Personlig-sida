Race Calendar

Here is the Formula 1 race schedule, one of the most important things to keep track of as a Formula 1 fan. It consists of 17 races, and takes place at 14 different race tracks, in 12 different countries. The schedule has gone through some big changes due to the current pandemic; there was originally meant to be 22 races in 22 different countries.

Tyres

Here is a list of all the different types of tyres that a team can put on their car. If you are new to Formula 1, it's good to know that the tyres are a very important part of the sport, since tyre choice and pit stops take a lot of strategy and heavily affect the outcome of a race.

Tyre Type Picture Brand Description
Soft Pirelli Slick tyre for dry conditions. Has the most grip but the lowest durability.
Medium Pirelli Slick tyre for dry conditions. Has medium grip and medium durability.
Hard Pirelli Slick tyre for dry conditions. Has the least grip but the highest durability.
Intermediate Pirelli Treaded tyre for wet conditions. Made to be used during light rain or after rain.
Wet Pirelli Treaded tyre for wet conditions. Made to be used during heavy rain.
